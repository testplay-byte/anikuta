## Key findings (the answer to the user's coupling question)
**Anime and manga are two PARALLEL, nearly-independent stacks — NOT coupled.**
- Source system: fully duplicated (`eu.kanade.tachiyomi.source` vs
  `...animesource`), NO shared base, disjoint APK features.
- Data layer: 2 separate SQLite files (`tachiyomi.db` + `tachiyomi.animedb`),
  separate drivers/handlers, zero shared tables.
- Download manager: fully split (`AnimeDownloadManager`/`MangaDownloadManager`),
  no shared base.
- Trackers: split `AnimeTracker`/`MangaTracker` (common base); 5 dual, 2
  anime-only, 4 manga-only.
- Player: anime-specific, only 2 trivial one-way manga imports (relocatable).
- Backup: unified protobuf container, split field numbers (manga 1-106,
  anime 500-507).
- Shared surface: theme/design system, DI (Injekt), Voyager nav, core utils,
  + 5 cross-cutting feeds (Library/History/Updates/Stats/Storage) that mix both.

**Conclusion:** anime-only is mechanically clean (deletion + DI pruning +
simplifying 5 feeds). Re-adding manga later is also mechanical IF we keep our
layout close to aniyomi's (links Decision 1 to Decision 2).
